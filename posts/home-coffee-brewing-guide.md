## It's Not About Expensive Equipment
You don't need a $2,000 espresso machine to make excellent coffee. Most of the improvement comes from a handful of fundamentals.

### Grind Size Matters More Than You Think
A pour-over needs a medium-coarse grind, while espresso needs a fine grind. Using the wrong grind size is the single most common brewing mistake.

### Water Temperature
Aim for water between 195-205°F (90-96°C). Water that's too hot will over-extract and taste bitter; too cool and it under-extracts, tasting flat and sour.

### The Golden Ratio
A good starting ratio is 1:16 — one gram of coffee for every 16 grams of water. Adjust from there based on your taste preference.

### Freshness Is Non-Negotiable
Coffee begins losing flavor within days of grinding. Buy whole beans and grind just before brewing for a noticeably fresher cup.

## Experiment and Adjust
Once you understand these fundamentals, the fun part begins — tweaking ratios, grind sizes, and brew times until you find your personal perfect cup.
